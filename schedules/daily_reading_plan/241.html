Job 27

1 Then Job again took up his discourse and said,

2 As God lives, who has taken away my right, / And the Almighty, who has embittered my soul,

3 As long as my breath is in me / And the spirit of God is in my nostrils,

4 Surely my lips will not speak anything wrong, / Nor will my tongue utter deceit.

5 Far be it from me to declare you righteous! / Until I die, I will not put away my integrity from me.

6 To my righteousness I will hold fast, I will not let it go; / My heart does not reproach me for any of my days.

7 May my enemy be like a wicked man, / And may he who rises up against me be like someone unjust.

8 For what is the hope of the profane man when God cuts him off, / When He takes away his soul?

9 Will God hear his cry / When distress comes upon him?

10 Does he take delight in the Almighty? / Will he call upon God at all times?

11 I will teach you about the hand of God; / What is with the Almighty I will not conceal.

12 Indeed, all of you have seen it yourselves; / And why then have you become altogether vain?

13 This is the portion with God for a wicked man, / And the inheritance that oppressors receive from the Almighty:

14 If his children are multiplied, it is for the sword; / And his offspring will not be satisfied with bread;

15 Those who remain of him will be buried in death, / And his widows will not lament.

16 Though he heaps up silver like dust / And prepares garments like clay,

17 He may prepare them, but the righteous will wear them; / And the innocent will divide the silver.

18 He builds his house as a moth does / And like a hut that a watchman makes.

19 He lies down rich but will be rich no more; / He opens his eyes, and he is not.

20 Terrors overtake him like waters; / At night a whirlwind steals him away.

21 The east wind carries him off, and he goes; / And it sweeps him out of his place.

22 And God hurls at him and does not spare; / He hastily flees from His hand.

23 Men clap their hands at him / And hiss him out of his place.
