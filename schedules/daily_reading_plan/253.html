Job 39

1 Do you know the time when the mountain goats bring forth? / Can you mark the time when the hinds calve?

2 Can you number the months that they must fulfill? / Or do you know the time when they bring forth?

3 They crouch down; they give birth to their young ones; / They send forth their offspring.

4 Their young become strong; they grow up in the open field; / They go forth and do not return to them.

5 Who has sent out the wild ass free? / Or who has loosed the bonds of the swift ass,

6 Whose home I have made the wilderness, / And the salt land, his dwelling place?

7 He scorns the tumult of the city; / He does not hear the shouts of the driver.

8 He searches out the mountains for his pasture / And seeks after every green thing.

9 Will the wild ox be willing to serve you / Or spend the night by your manger?

10 Can you bind the wild ox to the furrows with his ropes? / Or will he plow the valleys behind you?

11 Will you trust in him because his strength is great? / Or will you leave your labor to him?

12 Will you have confidence in him that he will bring in your seed / And gather your grain to your threshing floor?

13 The wings of the ostrich flap joyously: / Are they the pinion and plumage of lovingkindness?

14 For she leaves her eggs on the earth / And warms them in the dust.

15 And she forgets that a foot may crush them / Or that an animal of the field may trample them.

16 She is hard with her children, as if they were not hers; / Though her labor may be in vain, she has no fear;

17 Because God has caused her to forget wisdom / And has not appointed understanding to her.

18 At that time when she lifts herself up on high, / She scorns the horse and its rider.

19 Have you given the horse his might? / Have you clothed his neck with the quivering mane?

20 Have you made him to leap like a locust? / His majestic snorting is terrible.

21 He paws in the valley, / And he rejoices in his strength; / He goes forth to meet weapons.

22 He laughs at fear and is not dismayed, / And he does not turn back from the sword.

23 A quiver of arrows rattles against him, / The flashing spear and the javelin.

24 With fierceness and rage he swallows the ground, / And he cannot stand still at the sound of the trumpet.

25 As often as the trumpet sounds, he says, Aha! / And from afar he smells the battle, / The thundering of captains and the shouting.

26 Is it by your understanding that the hawk soars, / Stretching his wings to the south?

27 Is it at your command that the eagle mounts up / And makes his nest on high?

28 On the cliff he dwells and makes his lodging, / Upon the point of the cliff and the stronghold.

29 From there he spies out food; / His eyes gaze on it from afar.

30 And his young ones suck up the blood; / And where the slain are, there he is.
