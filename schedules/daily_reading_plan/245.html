Job 31

1 I made a covenant with my eyes; / How then can I gaze upon a virgin?

2 What then is the portion from God above, / Or the inheritance of the Almighty on high?

3 Is it not calamity for the unjust / And misfortune for the workers of iniquity?

4 Does He not see my ways / And count all my steps?

5 If I have walked with falsehood, / And my foot has hastened after deceit —

6 Let Him weigh me in a righteous balance, / And let God know my integrity —

7 If my step has turned from the way, / And my heart has gone after my own eyes, / And if any spot has stuck to my hands;

8 May I sow and another eat; / Indeed may my produce be rooted up.

9 If my heart has been enticed into following after a woman, / Or I have lain in wait at my neighbor’s door;

10 May my wife grind for another, / And may others kneel over her.

11 For that would have been a heinous act, / And it would be wickedness, to be punished by the judges.

12 For it is a fire that devours to Abaddon / And would root up all my increase.

13 If I have despised the cause of my servant or my maid / When they contended with me,

14 What then will I do when God rises up? / And when He visits me, what will I answer Him?

15 Did not He who made me in the womb make him? / And was it not One who fashioned us in the womb?

16 If I have withheld the poor from their desire, / Or have let the eyes of the widow fail,

17 Or have eaten my morsel alone / Without the orphan eating of it —

18 Rather, from my youth he grew up with me as with a father, / And from my mother’s womb I guided the widow —

19 If I have seen someone perishing from lack of clothing / Or that the needy had no covering;

20 If his loins have not blessed me, / And he has not been made warm with the fleece of my sheep;

21 If I have raised my hand against the orphan / Because I saw that I had support among those in the gate;

22 May my shoulder blade fall from the shoulder, / And may my arm be broken at the elbow.

23 For calamity from God is dreadful to me, / And because of His majesty I can do nothing.

24 If I have made gold my hope, / And have called fine gold my confidence;

25 If I have rejoiced because my wealth was great / And because my hand had acquired much;

26 If I have looked at the sun when it shone / Or the moon going on in splendor,

27 And my heart has been secretly enticed, / And my mouth has kissed my hand;

28 It too would be wickedness, to be punished by the judges, / For I would have denied God above.

29 If I have rejoiced at the misfortune of him who hated me, / Or have exulted when evil found him —

30 Rather, I have not allowed my mouth to sin / By asking for his life with a curse —

31 If the men of my tent have not said, / Who can find one who has not been filled with our master’s meat? —

32 The sojourner has not lodged in the street; / I have opened my doors to the highway —

33 If I have covered my transgressions as Adam did / By hiding my iniquity in my bosom,

34 Because I so dreaded the great multitude, / And the contempt of the families so frightened me, / That I was silent and did not go out my door —

35 Oh, that I had someone to hear me! / Here is my signature! Let the Almighty answer me. / And let my accuser write up the charge.

36 Surely I would carry it on my shoulder; / I would bind it onto me like a crown;

37 I would declare to Him the number of my steps; / Like a prince I would approach Him!

38 If my land cries out against me, / And its furrows weep together;

39 If I have eaten its strength without money, / And have caused its owners to lose their life;

40 May thorns come forth instead of wheat, / And pungent weeds instead of barley. / The words of Job are ended.
