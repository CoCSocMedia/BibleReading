Job 18

1 Then Bildad the Shuhite answered and said,

2 How long will you hunt for words? / Consider, and afterward we will speak.

3 Why are we considered to be like beasts / And have become unclean in your eyes?

4 You who tear yourself in your anger, / Will the earth be forsaken for your sake, / Or will the rock be removed from its place?

5 Indeed the light of the wicked goes out, / And the flame of his fire does not shine.

6 The light is darkness in his tent, / And his lamp above him goes out.

7 The steps of his strength are confined, / And his counsel has cast him down.

8 For he has been cast into a net by his own feet, / And he walks about on the webbing of a pitfall.

9 A snare grabs him by the heel; / A trap lays hold on him.

10 A rope is hidden for him on the ground, / And a trap for him, on the path.

11 Terrors frighten him all around / And chase him at his heels.

12 His strength is famished, / And calamity is prepared at his side.

13 It devours the members of his body; / The firstborn of death devours his members.

14 He is rooted out of his tent, in which he trusts; / And he is made to march to the king of terrors.

15 That which is not his dwells in his tent; / Brimstone is scattered upon his habitation.

16 Beneath, his roots are dried up; / And above, his branch is withered.

17 The memory of him perishes from the earth, / And he has no name on the open plain.

18 He is thrust out from the light to the darkness / And driven from the world.

19 He has no posterity and no progeny among his people, / And there are none remaining where he sojourned.

20 Those who come after will be astonished at his day, / As those who went before were horrified.

21 Surely these are the dwellings of the unjust, / And this is the place of him who does not know God.
