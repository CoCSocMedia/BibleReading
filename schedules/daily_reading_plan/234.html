Job 20

1 Then Zophar the Naamathite answered and said,

2 Because of this, my disquieting thoughts answer me; / And hence my haste is in me.

3 I hear the reproof that humiliates me, / And the spirit of my understanding answers me.

4 Do you not know this from of old, / Since man was set upon the earth,

5 That the joyous shouting of the wicked is short, / And the rejoicing of the profane is but for a moment?

6 Though his arrogance goes up to heaven, / And his head touches the clouds;

7 Like his own dung he perishes forever; / Those who have seen him say, Where is he?

8 Like a dream he flies away and is not found; / Indeed he is chased away like a vision of the night.

9 The eye looks on him then sees him no more, / Nor does his place observe him anymore.

10 His children seek the favor of the poor, / And his hands return his wealth.

11 His bones are full of youthful vigor, / Yet it lies down with him in the dust.

12 Though wickedness is sweet in his mouth, / Though he hides it under his tongue,

13 Though he favors it and will not forsake it, / But holds it in his mouth;

14 His food in his bowels is changed; / It is the venom of asps within him.

15 He swallows down riches and vomits them up; / God casts them forth from his stomach.

16 He sucks the poison of asps; / The tongue of the viper slays him.

17 He will not look on the rivers, / The streams flowing with honey and butter.

18 He will return what he toiled for and will not swallow it down; / And he will not rejoice according to the wealth of his trading.

19 For he has oppressed and abandoned the poor; / He has violently seized a house that he did not build.

20 Because he knew no respite in his craving, / Of that which he desired he will save nothing.

21 There will be nothing left of what he has devoured; / Thus his prosperity will not endure.

22 In the fullness of his sufficiency he will be distressed; / The hand of everyone in trouble will come against him.

23 In order to fill his belly, / God will send the burning fierceness of His wrath on him / And will rain it upon him as his food.

24 He will flee from the iron weapon, / But the bronze bow will strike him through.

25 He draws the arrow out, and it comes out of his body; / Indeed the glittering point goes forth from his gall. / Terrors come upon him.

26 Total darkness is laid up as his treasures; / A fire not fanned by man will devour him; / It will feed on what is left in his tent.

27 The heavens will reveal his iniquity, / And the earth will rise up against him.

28 The increase of his house will depart / As things swept away in the day of His wrath.

29 This is the wicked man’s portion from God / And the inheritance decreed to him by God.
