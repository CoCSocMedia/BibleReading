Job 22

1 And Eliphaz the Temanite answered and said,

2 Can a man of might be of any use to God? / No, a man of insight is of use only to himself.

3 Is it a matter of delight to the Almighty that you are righteous? / Or is it a gain to Him that you make your ways perfect?

4 Is it because of your fear of Him that He reproves you, / That He enters into judgment with you?

5 Is not your wickedness great, / And is there no end to your iniquities?

6 For you have taken pledges from your brothers without cause / And stripped the naked of their clothing.

7 You have not given water to the weary to drink, / And you have withheld bread from the hungry.

8 And as for the man of power, the earth was his; / And the man of honor dwelt in it.

9 You have sent away the widows empty, / And the arms of the orphans have been crushed.

10 Therefore snares are all around you; / And sudden dread troubles you,

11 Or darkness, so that you cannot see; / And an abundance of waters covers you.

12 Is not God at the height of heaven? / And look at the highest of the stars, how lofty they are!

13 And you say, What does God know? / Can He judge through the deep darkness?

14 Thick clouds are a covering to Him, so He cannot see; / And He walks upon the circle of the heavens.

15 Will you keep the old way, / Which wicked men have trodden?

16 Such were snatched away before their time, / Whose foundation was poured forth like a stream;

17 Who said to God, Depart from us; / And, What can the Almighty accomplish for us?

18 Yet He fills their houses with good things. / But the counsel of the wicked is far from Him.

19 The righteous see and rejoice, / And the innocent deride them,

20 Saying, Surely those who rose up against us are cut off, / And the remnant of them the fire has devoured.

21 Be well disposed to Him and at peace with Him; / By such, good will come upon you.

22 Receive instruction from His mouth, / And lay up His words in your heart.

23 If you return to the Almighty, you will be built up. / If you put injustice far away from your tents,

24 And place your gold nuggets in the dust / And your gold of Ophir in the stones of the brooks,

25 Then the Almighty will be your gold nuggets / And precious silver to you.

26 For then you will delight yourself in the Almighty, / And you will lift up your countenance to God.

27 You will pray to Him, and He will hear you; / And you will repay your vows.

28 You will also decree something, and it will be established for you; / And light will shine on your ways.

29 When they cast you down, you will say, I am up! / And the lowly person He will save.

30 He will deliver him who is not innocent; / Yes, you will be delivered through the cleanness of your hands.
